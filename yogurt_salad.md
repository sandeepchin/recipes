
# Yogurt Salad Recipe

## Custom recipe by Sandeep Chintabathina

This is a classic yogurt salad recipe that goes with Chicken or Mutton (lamb or goat) biryani. Although not an original recipe, I have customized it to match my taste and availability of ingredients. In my native language, Telugu, this dish is called Perugu Pacchadi.

List of ingredients (for 4+ servings)

- 32 oz whole milk plain yogurt
- 1 cucumber
- 2 medium sized tomatoes or 1 large tomato
- 1 shallot or 1/4 of a red onion
- 1 serrano or Thai chili pepper (optional)
- 1 large lime or lemon
- 2 cilantro sprigs 
- 2 mint sprigs
- sea salt 
- black pepper

## Procedure

1. Peel the cucumber and dice it into small pieces. Place it in bowl.
2. Dice the tomatoes and onion into small pieces. Add it to the bowl.
3. Slit and dice the chili pepper and add it the bowl. The pepper adds a kick to the salad and is optional.
4. Squeeze the lime juice into the bowl. Make sure seeds are filtered out.
5. Chop the cilantro stems but leave the leaves as they are and add both to the bowl.
6. Pluck mint leaves and add to the bowl.
7. Add salt usually 1 table spoon. The cucumber releases water so this should be okay.
8. Add black pepper to taste.
9. Empty yogurt into the bowl and mix everything together. Add some water if it is too thick.
10. Refrigerate and serve cold or at room temperature with Biryani or another spicy dish.

The dish tastes even better the next day as all the vegetables release their flavor over time.
